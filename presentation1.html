What is the Fading American Dream

The fading american dream is looking at the downfall of what America is suppose to represent. We called the "Land of opportunity" and despite that title the opportunities we actually have seem o diminish daily.

The United States of America is a global superpower and countries are constantly observing us to see how the world will be shaped this is import to everyone's livelihood as governments are supposed to protect us and have order as social contract theory dictates

In 2020 there was the covid-19 Pandemic which caused a mass layoff percentage around the country. SO if unemployment has gone up how did the the pricing of houses increase by so much.

Investors and corporations would the responsible party for this. As Companies such as Blackrock and Vanguard starting buying houses and covering their tracks through media manipulation the got into bidding wars with average families to acquire homes.

The real estate tax code allows for income that comes from homes is so generous to landlords that many multi-millionaire investors took the opportunity of lower interest meant for middle class families to purchase homes to rent out for increased

Applying Structural Functionalism

This approach would try to fix the problem while keeping capitalism in tact

Here's a list of possible solutions that we could apply to help fix the issue:

  • Not allow corporations or LLCs to purchase homes. This would stop corporations from sweeping the housing market
  • Update real estate tax code. disincentive multi-millionaires from obtaining new more than a certain number of homes.
  • regulate housing costs. Create a limit on how much houses can cost per sqft and add in variables to make costs fair but affordable.
  • Increase wages so that people can actually acquire downpayments to get their own homes.
